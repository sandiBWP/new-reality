# False Binaries Create False Choices

Many of the systems shaping modern life were built for an era that rewarded scale, efficiency, globalization, and optimization. To achieve those goals, we learned to think in tradeoffs.

Growth **OR** Resilience. Returns **OR** Impact. Capital **OR** Community. Global **OR** Local. Investor **OR** Citizen.

For decades, these choices appeared necessary. But what if many of them are not choices at all? What if they are the result of failures of imagination — and missing mechanisms?

---

## The Most Common False Binaries

### Practical vs. Adaptable

"Be practical." It's advice everyone has received. But practical according to whom? In which reality?

The choices that were practical in 1985 — buy a house, stay with one employer, trust the pension — were rational for that world. Continuing to make those same choices when the world has changed isn't practicality. It's inertia disguised as prudence.

The opposite of practical may not be reckless. It may be **adaptable**.

### Growth vs. Resilience

For decades, growth was the objective. Efficiency was the strategy. Scale was the reward.

But optimization for growth often removes the redundancy that creates resilience. A supply chain optimized for cost has no slack. A portfolio optimized for returns has no connection to place. A community optimized for efficiency has no buffer when conditions change.

The question isn't growth *or* resilience. The question is: can we build systems that produce both?

### Returns vs. Impact

The financial industry treats this as a trade-off: you can have competitive returns, or you can have positive impact, but not both.

This framing assumes that impact investments are inherently less efficient. But what if the reverse is true — what if the investments disconnected from real economic outcomes are the ones carrying hidden risk?

### Global vs. Local

The world spent 40 years globalizing. Capital flowed to where returns were highest. Supply chains stretched across oceans. Decision-making concentrated in a few global centers.

Now the world is regionalizing. Different places are experiencing fundamentally different realities. The investment products haven't caught up.

The choice was never global *or* local. It was: centralized management with decentralized deployment.

### Investor vs. Citizen

You are told these are separate roles. In one, you maximize returns. In the other, you care about outcomes.

But this separation is a design choice, not a law of nature. When your retirement savings fund things that harm your community, you haven't succeeded as an investor *or* a citizen. You've been forced into a trade-off that doesn't need to exist.

---

## Why This Matters

False binaries aren't just intellectual errors. They shape:

- **Policy**: If we believe growth and resilience are opposites, we'll never design for both
- **Investment products**: If we believe returns and impact can't coexist, we'll never build structures that deliver both
- **Personal decisions**: If we believe practical and meaningful are in tension, we'll keep choosing practical — and wonder why it doesn't feel right

The New Reality begins with a simple observation: Many of today's biggest challenges persist because we continue to accept false choices where better systems could create both.

The first step isn't picking a side. It's noticing there was never supposed to be sides at all.
