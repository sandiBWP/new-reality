# Regional Funds

The New Reality Fund uses a centralized management structure to enable regional deployment at scale.

The model is designed to be repeatable: one national management platform supporting multiple regional BDCs. Each fund invests in businesses and assets specific to its region's needs.

---

## Why Regions Matter Again

For the last forty years, capital was rewarded for scale and efficiency. Regional resilience was largely an afterthought.

Today, many of the challenges people care about are regional. Housing, energy, infrastructure, and labor markets vary dramatically by location. Different places are experiencing fundamentally different economic realities.

A one-size-fits-all investment product can't address these differences. Regional funds can.

---

## How It Works

**NRF Management** provides centralized oversight, strategy, and infrastructure. Functions include underwriting, compliance, technology, investor relations, distribution, operations, and marketing.

**Regional BDCs** deploy capital under this umbrella. Each regional fund has its own investment focus, tailored to the specific needs and opportunities of its region.

**Future Expansion** follows the same model. Once the infrastructure is built for one region, deploying to additional regions becomes a repeatable process — not a new project from scratch.

---

## First Deployments

**California** — The first regional deployment. California's diverse economy spans technology, agriculture, manufacturing, and services. Focus areas include housing, healthcare, food systems, energy, and industrial services.

**Western North Carolina** — Second planned deployment. A different regional profile — smaller communities, significant small business density, and growing interest in local economic resilience.

---

## The Paradox

To make investing local, the infrastructure must be national. One audience. One management platform. Many regions. This is the insight that makes regional investing scalable for the first time.
